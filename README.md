# Astrea
Astrea(Astrophysics nuclear Synthesis and Theoretical Reaction Evaluation Agent) is a ai4s engine linking microscopic nuclear physics to macroscopic astrophysics.It can automatically generate PRL-caliber research proposals and precise experimental blueprints for high-impact nuclear measurements

## Project Overview

In nuclear astrophysics, minor uncertainties in microscopic nuclear properties—such as resonance energies, stellar reaction rates, or astrophysical $S$-factors—can trigger cascading, non-linear impacts on macroscopic cosmic events, including core-collapse supernovae, X-ray bursts, and $r$-process nucleosynthesis. However, identifying which specific nuclear reaction holds the key to a particular astrophysical puzzle often transcends the cognitive synthesis boundaries of individual researchers.

*ASTREA* is a domain-specific, intelligent reasoning system built to solve this bottleneck. Acting as an automated co-pilot for nuclear astrophysicists, the system integrates a Multi-Agent cognitive architecture with GraphRAG (Knowledge Graph Enhanced Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to achieve deep physical reasoning.

## Key Capabilities:

By cross-synthesizing peer-reviewed literature and deploying deep-reasoning multi-agent workflows, ASTREA uncovers hidden non-linear connections between micro-nuclear parameters and cosmic anomalies, accelerating scientific discovery by generating PRL-caliber research proposals and actionable experimental directives.

*Cross-Disciplinary Synthesis*: 
Ingests and contextualizes highly dense, formula-heavy literature across authoritative journals (PRL, PRC, ApJ, etc.), while dynamically mapping and importing mathematical and theoretical tools from adjacent physical domains.
 
*Contradiction & Sensitivity Evaluation*: 
Scans global nuclear databases (e.g., EXFOR, JINA REACLIB) to isolate conflicting experimental data and evaluates their sensitivity against stellar evolutionary models to locate high-value scientific targets.
  
*PRL-Caliber Proposal Forging*: 
Synthesizes multi-hop physical reasoning into rigorous, high-impact research hypotheses. It automatically generates end-to-end project proposals, guiding experimentalists with concrete measurement strategies (e.g., optimal energy windows, kinematic setups, and reaction channels) to rapidly validate the theory in the lab.
